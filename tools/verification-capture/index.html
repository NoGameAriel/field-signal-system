Core Idea
Verification breaks when the verifier depends on the entity being verified.
This is not about corruption or bad actors. It is about what the structure makes likely — regardless of anyone's intentions.

Common Signs of Capture
```
Unusually high pass rates across diverse cases
When everything passes, the check may not be checking.
Very low disagreement between reviewers
Healthy systems produce variance. Uniformity is a warning.
Language that softens over time
"Concerns noted" replacing "failed." Hedging replacing specificity.
Problems only surface after external exposure
Corrections that require crisis are corrections the system suppressed.
Stability in outcomes even as reality changes
Captured systems lag. The signal stays clean until it suddenly isn't.
★ Key Signal
Everything looks consistently fine.
```
Diagnostic
The Four Questions
Use these anywhere — no expertise required
```
01
Who funds the verifier?
If the money traces back to the entity being verified — or to an ecosystem that benefits from favorable outcomes — the signal is at structural risk.
02
Who controls their future work?
Referrals, reputation, reappointment — these are leverage. Even clean payment doesn't protect independence if access is conditional.
03
How quickly does reality correct the system?
If corrections only arrive through crisis or external pressure, the internal feedback loop is not functioning.
04
Is disagreement preserved?
Healthy verification produces variance. When disagreement disappears, that uniformity is itself a signal worth examining.
Quick Read
If funding and future control both point back to the subject, the verification is structurally compromised — regardless of anyone's intentions.
```
What Clean Looks Like
Independence is structural — not personal.
```
✓
Funding is separated from the subject being verified
✓
Verifiers are assigned externally, randomly, or rotated
✓
No single actor controls both outcomes and evaluator survival
✓
Disagreement is visible, preserved, and treated as signal
✓
Results are published where neither party can edit post-hoc
A good verifier in a captured system still produces captured outcomes. The structure matters more than the individual.
```
